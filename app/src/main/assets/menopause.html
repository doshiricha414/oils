Menopause is the natural rebalancing of hormones in a woman’s body as she transitions to the after-child-bearing years. This period varies from individual to individual starting as early as the mid 40s to as late as the mid 50s. The technical definition of menopause is the point in a woman’s life when she has not had a menstrual period for 1 year. Perimenopause (pre-menopause) is a period of 2 to 8 years that leads to menopause. During this transition there can be a variety of symptoms from the common hot flash to physical and emotional concerns. The changes themselves all stem from the hormonal rebalancing that naturally occurs in the body at this time.
