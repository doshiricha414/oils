Child Birth

After months of anticipation a new life will come into the world. A mother’s anticipation and preparation come together in the miracle of childbirth. A pregnancy normally takes 38 to 42 weeks measured from the first day of the last menstrual period (or 266 days plus or minus 2 weeks from the time of fertilization). Here is a brief overview of what to expect just prior to and during childbirth. 

Signs of Labor

•  Baby “drops” also known as “lightening”. This is when the baby’s head drops down into your pelvis in preparation for delivery relieving pressure on the ribcage . May be a few weeks before labor begins.

•  More Braxton Hicks contractions. Although Braxton Hicks contractions may occur as early as the second trimester if they become more frequent and intense it can be a signal of pre-labor. (Braxton Hicks or false labor contractions differ from real contractions in that they are irregular in intensity and rhythm, infrequent, for most more uncomfortable than painful and do not increase in frequency or intensity but rather taper off and then disappear.)

•  A bloody or brownish discharge will pass, sometimes as a lump, sometimes as a discharge over several days. This is the mucus plug that has sealed off the womb from infection that passes days before up to the onset of labor. This is also known as “bloody show”.

•  Some experience frequent and loose stools, diarrhea, just prior to labor.

•  As labor is imminent (at least within 24 hours) the "water breaks". This is the term to describe when the amniotic sac that surrounds the baby ruptures. .

•  Contractions begin that are at periodic intervals less than 10 minutes apart.

Stages of Labor

Stage 1.

 Latent, active, and transition phases.

•  Latent labor is the first phase as contractions become fore frequent and the cervix begins to dilate to approximately 3 to 4 centimeters.

•  Active labor follows as the cervix dilates up to 7 centimeters with more pressure on the back and abdomen with each contraction.

•  Transition labor then follows as the cervix fully dilates to 10 centimeters, contractions are strong and more frequent coming every 3 to 4 minutes and lasting 60 to 90 seconds.

Stage 2.

With the cervix completely open pushing and contractions will bring the baby through the birth canal. Normal delivery begins with the top of the baby’s head first aided be the fontanels (soft spots) allowing it to pass through the narrow canal. Then the baby’s head crowns, meaning the widest point of the head reaches the vaginal opening. Continued pushing then delivers the baby’s shoulders and body and the baby clears and the umbilical cord separation can proceed.

Stage 3. 

With the baby delivered the placenta then passes through the birth canal and is the final stage of this marvelous process.

Suggested protocols:

Delivery

Baby position

Some report that Myrrh and/or Peppermint with FCO applied to abdomen may help turn a baby that is breach.

Calming during early labor

Massage Blend, Grounding Blend, Clary Sage with Geranium or Lavender are among those mentioned to help if a calming influence is needed. With FCO applied to back and shoulders or bottoms of the feet.

Labor room atmosphere

Diffuse lightly Lavender, Invigorating Blend, Grounding Blend or Wild Orange. Be certain the aroma is something soothing to the new mom.

Labor

Slowing early labor - A few drops of Lavender applied to the tummy.

More effective contractions - Clary Sage with Geranium or Lavender applied to inside of ankles, bottoms of feet, lower abdomen and lower back.

Intensify and move along - Myrrh or Clary Sage with Myrrh applied to inside of ankles, bottoms of feet, lower abdomen and lower back.

For pain - Basil or Black Pepper with FCO massaged on lower back.

Perineum care

Before delivery - Various authors suggest a spray or massage technique for a week to a month before delivery using Frankincense/Helichrysum, Geranium/Lavender or Clary Sage/Rose. (Please see Fritz or Worwood books for more details.)

During delivery - 10 drops Helichrysum or 5 drops each Helichrysum and Frankincense with 1 Tablespoon FCO gently massaged on perineum during crowning and on baby's head during birth.

 

Post delivery

After pain

If there are after pains (similar to menstrual cramps) 1 -3 drops of Massage Blend, Soothing Blend, Lavender or Solace are suggested to be applied directly to lower abdomen.

Anointing newborn

Many suggest a single drop of Frankincense be applied to the new child's crown and down their spine.

Breast feeding

see separate page on Breastfeeding.

Caesarean, prevent scarring

Anti-Aging Blend or Helichrysum/Lavender to area to promote healing and prevent scarring. Use topically, at least daily, until there is noticeable improvement.

Circumcision healing

Anti-Aging Blend or Frankincense/Lavender with FCO applied directly for healing.

Hemorrhoids

Helichrysum and/or Lavender apply topically 1- 2 drops of each oil directly to area of discomfort. Sitz baths as well may help, warm water in tub to cover hips with 2 -3 drops of oils.

Jaundice

Apply one drop each of Geranium and Lemon to the bottoms of the baby's feet every 3 - 4 hours.

Perineum care and healing episiotomies

Anti-Aging Blend or Frankincense/Lavender with FCO for healing applied directly or mixed with carrier or water and with a spray 3 - 4 times a day.

Postpartum depression

A number of single oils and combinations are suggested. Because individuals are so different it may require some experimentation to find the blend that is best. The application methods vary as well. Topically to the bottoms of the feet, diffusion, 1 -2 drops on the pillowcase or 5 - 6 drops in a bath are all suggested.

The Grounding Blend daily on the bottoms of the feet is a strong suggestion. Other single oils most often mentioned are Clary Sage, Frankincense, Rose and Ylang Ylang. Also mentioned are Bergamot, Joyful Blend, Geranium, Grapefruit, Lavender, Lemon, Myrrh, Sandalwood and Wild Orange.

Stephanie Fritz in her book mentions this blend along with a number of others: Geranium, Lavender, Sandalwood and Ylang Ylang. Valerie Ann Worwood also mentions a number of blends including 10 drops Bergamot,  5 drops Clary Sage and 2 drops Rose.

Umbilical cord

1 drop of Myrrh on and around stump for healing. Continue daily with 1 - 2 drops Frankincense till cord stump falls off (usually about a week).
