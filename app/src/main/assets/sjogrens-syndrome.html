Sjogren's Syndrome

Sjogren’s syndrome is an autoimmune disorder primarily recognized by the symptoms of dry eyes and dry mouth. Primary Sjogren’s is an instance of the disease when there is not associated illness, secondary Sjogren’s is the common situation when it is associated with another autoimmune disorder such as lupus, rheumatoid arthritis or scleroderma. Normally the immune system attacks the glands that make tears and saliva hence the common symptoms but often other parts of the body are affected with other painful symptoms. This can include joints, nerves, lungs, kidneys, circulatory and digestive systems. Those over 40 are most likely to have Sjogren’s and for no reported reason 90 percent are female.
