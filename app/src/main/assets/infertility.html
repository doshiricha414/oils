Infertility

Primary infertility is defined as not being able to get pregnant despite having frequent, unprotected sex for at least a year, or for at least six months if the woman is age 35 or older. Secondary infertility is a term referring to couples who have been pregnant at least once but have not been since. The cause for infertility may be with the woman, the man or both partners.

The most common causes among women that are mentioned include tumors, fibroids, polyps, cysts, defects in reproductive tract, thyroid disorders, candida infection, diabetes, hormonal disorders, PCOS, PID, obesity, excessive exercise, drugs, excessive alcohol, poor nutrition and age. Among men certain infections, environmental pollutants (radiation, etc), birth defects, certain pharmaceuticals, heavy use of drugs or alcohol and age. 

In a healthy couple under age 30 who have sex regularly, the chance of getting pregnant is about 25 - 30% per month. After the woman is age 35 – 40 the chances are reduced considerably.
