Uterine Fibroids

Uterine fibroids are benign (noncancerous) tumors that are found in the uterus.  There are a number of medical terms used to describe the various locations of the tumors within the uterus. (see diagram) The reason for the growth of these fibroid tumors is not understood but some suggest they are linked to the hormone estrogen.  They occur in the majority of women but often go undetected because they are very small or cause no noticeable symptoms. 

Typically if they develop during child bearing years there will be more than one and they will slowly grow. They can be a factor in heavy menstrual bleeding and/or menstrual discomfort but seldom are they a factor in uterine cancer or do they become cancerous tumors.
