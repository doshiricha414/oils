Shingles

Shingles, also know as herpes zoster, is a viral infection of the nerve roots.  The initial viral infection occurs with childhood chickenpox then lays dormant in the body for years and may resurface as shingles when the body is weakened from another disease or aging. 

The symptoms are much different than the original chickenpox.  Shingles is characterized by a very painful rash that can be anywhere on the body but most often will be on one side of the abdomen.  There will be symptoms prior to the rash, these may include headache, sensitivity to light, flu like discomfort with no fever.  The rash may progress to the point of pussy blisters and include pain and itching.
