Hemorrhage is the medical term to describe any type of bleeding. There are many types ranging from a small superficial cut in the skin to a severe traumatic injury involving a major artery. Hemorrhage can also be via one of the orifices in the body or can be in a variety of internal organs where the bleeding is not easily detected. We have listed below some of the more common types of hemorrhage. 

Minor traumatic bleeding. Small cuts and wounds are typically not dangerous and in most cases the bleeding will stop with a little assistance or on its own.

Severe traumatic bleeding. Depending on the blood vessel involved and the severity of the injury the dangers vary. Severe bleeding to a minor vein may be stopped by application of pressure where a major artery may require a temporary tourniquet and always medical attention.

Postoperative hemorrhage. Bleeding after surgery can occur anywhere such a procedure has taken place.

Externalized bleeding. This describes the class of hemorrhage where blood flows from one of the natural orifices in the body. Ears, nose, mouth, urethra, vagina or anus. These are usually caused by disease or trauma within the body leading to the bleeding detected in one of these locations. These would include occurrences such as:

•  Hemorrhoids 

•  Hematuria or blood in the urine from urinary bleeding

•  Hemoptysis or coughing up blood from the lungs

•  Menorrhagia or heavy menstrual bleeding

•  Nosebleeds

•  Postpartum hemorrhage from childbirth

Internal bleeding. This describes hemorrhages that occur within the body and may not be detected immediately. Again the bleeding may be from small capillaries, veins or arteries with varying degrees of concern. A few types include:

•  Brain hemorrhages including stroke, aneurysm, intracranial bleeding from a head injury, etc.

•  Hematemesis or bleeding within the stomach

•  Carcinoma (cancer) may cause bleeding in the gastro-intestinal tract or lungs and in some cases the small organs such as the kidney, pancreas or prostate.

•  A tubal pregnancy (ectopic) will result in internal bleeding
